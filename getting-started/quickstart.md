# Quick Start

This guide walks you through your first few minutes with MemoryCo — from setting up your identity to storing and recalling your first memories.

## Step 1: Set Up Your Identity

Identity is the foundation of MemoryCo. It defines *who your assistant is* when talking to you — their name, personality, values, and how they communicate.

Start by asking your assistant:

> "Let's set up your identity."

The assistant will walk you through an interactive onboarding process where you can define:

- **Persona** — A name and description for your assistant
- **Traits** — Personality characteristics
- **Values** — Principles that guide behavior
- **Preferences** — How they should approach tasks
- **Tone** — Communication style

Identity never decays. It's not memory — it's who your assistant *is*.

Identity isn't a one-time setup. You can refine it at any point — even mid-conversation. Just tell your assistant to update a trait, add a value, change the tone, or adjust anything else. It's designed to evolve as your working relationship develops.

## Step 2: Have a Conversation

Once identity is set up, just talk naturally. MemoryCo works in the background:

- Your assistant searches for relevant memories before responding
- New facts are stored automatically as atomic memories
- Memories referenced in conversation get reinforced (recalled)
- Unused memories gradually decay — that's by design

## Step 3: Check What's Stored

Ask your assistant:

> "What do you know about me?"
> "Search your memories for [topic]"

## Step 4: Explore the Dashboard

MemoryCo includes a visual dashboard for browsing memories, associations, and the knowledge graph. Ask your assistant:

> "Open the memory dashboard."

The dashboard opens in your browser and gives you a direct view into what your assistant knows — you can explore connections between memories, check energy levels, and see how your knowledge graph is growing.
